About Me

Hello! My research focuses on document analysis and low-resource document processing.

From 2011 to 2016, I led the research and development of the first Khmer Semantic Search Engine and an Open Educational Resources system for Khmer low-resource document retrieval, ontology development, and document processing. This work was made possible through collaborations and funding from the Institute of Technology of Cambodia (ITC), the Ministry of Education, Youth and Sport (MoEYS) in Cambodia, and ARES (Belgium).

From 2018 to 2025, I expanded my research into historical document analysis, covering almost all key tasks in document analysis, including dataset creation, layout analysis, enhancement, classification, and text recognition. I collaborated with institutions including the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), the Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS), the University of Mons (Belgium), and FAU Erlangen-Nürnberg (Germany) on PALM-SEA, a research project dedicated to historical palm-leaf manuscripts across Southeast Asia, including: Khmer (Cambodia): Sleukrith and archive collections, Sundanese (Indonesia, Java): 15th-century manuscripts and Balinese (Indonesia, Bali). Later on, PALM-GLOBAL introduced cross-domain palm-leaf manuscript analysis across six scripts from South and Southeast Asia, including: Tamil, Malayalam, Kambaramayanam, Khmer, Balinese, and Sundanese.

From 2025–present, we further extended the project with new collaborations involving researchers from Cambodia, NERC-SLIP(China), the University Paris Cité (France) to address emerging challenges in Ancient Khmer Stone Inscriptions Analysis (6th–16th centuries). Details will be available soon, with implications for cultural heritage preservation.

Alongside work on ancient scripts, I also recently focused on multimodal document analysis for non-Latin, low-resource modern documents in Khmer, Burmese, Lao, Chinese, and Japanese. My goals are to create new datasets for non-Latin and underexplored documents and develop and evaluate document understanding methods, such as layout analysis, OCR, and VQA—especially in combination with LLM-based models for stronger multimodal document understanding. However, the updated results will be published after relevant papers and tools are accepted.

"My writing may be long, but I am still young as a researcher and lack a lot of experience. I remain eager to gain more experience and continue learning from experts. I warmly welcome researchers interested in these fields to reach out to discuss ideas or explore collaborations."
