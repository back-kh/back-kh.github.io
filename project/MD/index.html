Document Understanding for Non-Latin Low-Resource Documents

Building robust document-understanding tools to facilitate accurate information extraction and retrieval for complex, non-Latin scripts. This research targets critical gaps in multimodal document analysis, specifically focusing on Khmer, Burmese, Lao, Chinese, and Japanese documents.

Paper 1: Layout Analysis

Published Research

KH-FUNSD: A Hierarchical and Fine-Grained Layout Analysis Dataset

To establish a foundation for low-resource document understanding, we developed KH-FUNSD. This dataset addresses the severe lack of resources for analyzing the spatial layout of Khmer business documents.

Unlike standard Latin documents, Khmer scripts present unique challenges due to zero-width spacing, complex consonant clusters, and distinct typographical structures. KH-FUNSD provides a hierarchical, fine-grained annotation schema that allows deep learning models to accurately perform structural layout analysis (identifying headers, key-value pairs, lists, and tables) in highly complex business forms.

Architecture & Methodology

Building upon our success in layout analysis, the current frontier of this research shifts towards Visual Question Answering (VQA) on multimodal documents. We are engineering systems that do not merely parse document structure, but genuinely "understand" the semantic relationships between text, spatial layout, and visual elements in non-Latin documents.

Proposed Multimodal Fusion Architecture for Non-Latin Document VQA.

Spatial-Aware Multimodal Fusion

Traditional NLP models fail on visually-rich documents because they process text as a 1D sequence. Our framework employs a multi-stream architecture that dynamically fuses visual embeddings (extracted via Vision Transformers from document images), linguistic embeddings (from multilingual encoders adapted for complex tokenization), and spatial coordinates (bounding box geometries). This allows the model to answer layout-dependent queries, such as reading values from specific columns in a Khmer or Lao table.

Addressing Low-Resource Typographical Complexity

Scripts like Khmer, Burmese, and Lao utilize non-linear writing systems where vowels and diacritics stack above, below, or around base consonants. Our research introduces specialized cross-attention mechanisms that handle these zero-width characters and complex syllable boundaries without losing spatial context, a severe limitation in current state-of-the-art multi-modal models trained predominantly on Latin documents.

Cross-lingual Document Retrieval

Beyond direct question answering, the embedded multimodal representations are being leveraged to enhance cross-lingual search. This capability aims to dramatically improve search engines' ability to retrieve specific data points, invoices, or forms directly from scanned native-script PDFs across Southeast Asian languages.
