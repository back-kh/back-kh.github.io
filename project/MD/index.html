Document Understanding for Non-Latin Low-Resource Documents

Project Lead: Nimol Thuon

Building robust document-understanding tools to facilitate accurate information extraction and retrieval for complex, non-Latin scripts. This research targets critical gaps in multimodal document analysis, specifically focusing on Khmer, Burmese, Lao, Chinese, and Japanese documents.

Paper 1: Layout Analysis

Published Research

KH-FUNSD: A Hierarchical and Fine-Grained Layout Analysis Dataset

To establish a foundation for low-resource document understanding, we developed KH-FUNSD. This dataset addresses the severe lack of resources for analyzing the spatial layout of Khmer business documents.

Unlike standard Latin documents, Khmer scripts present unique challenges due to zero-width spacing, complex consonant clusters, and distinct typographical structures. KH-FUNSD provides a hierarchical, fine-grained annotation schema that allows deep learning models to accurately perform structural layout analysis (identifying headers, key-value pairs, lists, and tables) in highly complex business forms.

Current & Future Directions

Expanding to Multimodal Visual VQA

Under Review 2 papers detailing our multimodal Visual VQA framework for low-resource languages are currently under peer review.

Building upon our success in layout analysis, the current frontier of this research shifts towards Visual Question Answering (VQA) on multimodal documents. We aim to create systems that do not just parse structure, but genuinely "understand" the semantic relationship between text, layout, and visual elements in non-Latin documents.

  • Target Languages: Expanding methodologies developed for Khmer to include Lao and Burmese, creating a unified framework for Southeast Asian low-resource scripts.
  • Multimodal Fusion: Integrating visual embeddings (document images) with linguistic embeddings (complex non-Latin text) for high-accuracy VQA.
  • Cross-lingual Document Retrieval: Enhancing search engines' ability to retrieve specific information directly from scanned documents or PDFs in native scripts.

Research in Progress (Coming soon...)

The advanced framework for Visual VQA and comprehensive Layout Analysis across Khmer, Lao, and Burmese is currently in active development. New datasets, methodologies, and the corresponding publications are coming soon by the authors.
