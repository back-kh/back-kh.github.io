Technical Overview & Methodology

The digitization and computational analysis of ancient Khmer stone epigraphy present highly complex computer vision challenges. Unlike standard paper-based historical documents, stone inscriptions suffer from severe material degradation, non-planar topological surfaces, biological weathering (such as lichen and moss), and arbitrary spatial carving layouts. Furthermore, the archaic Khmer script has evolved significantly over a millennium, requiring specialized few-shot learning approaches.

Image Enhancement & Topographical Normalization

Traditional binarization techniques (e.g., Sauvola, Otsu) fail on stone textures due to extreme variations in illumination and depth. Our proposed methodology involves deep learning-based texture filtering and illumination correction networks. These models are designed to decouple the 3D carving depth and lighting artifacts from the actual 2D stroke topology, effectively isolating the carved graphemes from the background stone anomalies.

Instance Segmentation on Unconstrained Canvases

Ancient stone carvers did not strictly adhere to linear baselines. To address the irregular geometry of the text, we deploy custom Transformer-based segmentation architectures. These models perform instance-level text-line extraction and bounding-polygon generation, mapping the flow of the script across cracks, breaks, and heavily eroded sections of the stele.

Low-Resource Archaic Script Recognition

Recognizing characters that span from the pre-Angkorian (6th century) to the post-Angkorian periods requires robust intra-class variance handling. We are developing low-resource and few-shot Optical Character Recognition (OCR) models that bridge the topological features of ancient archaic characters with their modern Khmer equivalents, enabling automated transcription and semantic analysis of these cultural artifacts.

Keywords: Stone Epigraphy, Optical Character Recognition (OCR), Topology Normalization, Zero-shot Learning
